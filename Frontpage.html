Financial Management Information System

FMIS Cambodia



Enhancing Cambodia's Public Finance with Transparency, Accountability, and Efficiency

Why FMIS?

The purpose of implementing the FMIS system is to decentralize technology use for public financial management in both national and sub-national administrations, as well as expedite the implementation of financial procedures for increased efficiency and effectiveness. With the complete implementation of procedures within the system, FMIS will accelerate and facilitate the timely provision of public services to citizens. It is positioned as a crucial tool for the Royal Government of Cambodia in effectively managing revenue-expenditure information, contributing to the transformation of Cambodia into a high-middle-income country by 2030 and a high-income country by 2050.

Background of FMIS Implementation

The inception of the Financial Management Information System (FMIS) dates back to 2008, when it was introduced to bolster the objectives of the Public Financial Reform Program (PFMRP). The second phase of PFMRP aimed to enhance financial accountability. The FMIS project underwent continuous refinement, culminating in its official launch on January 21, 2014.

Overview of FMIS Scope

Following the Royal Government of Cambodia's successful completion of the initial phase of the Public Financial Management Reform Program (PFMRP) in 2008, the Ministry of Economy and Finance embarked on the extensive planning of the Financial Management Information System (FMIS) project. This large-scale initiative encompasses both functional aspects and construction sites, aligning with the directives and guidance from development partners, notably the World Bank.

Project Suspension Due to Supplier Issues

By the close of 2011, the project proposal had faced suspension owing to issues related to the supply chain.

FMIS as a Small-Scale Treasury System Project

In 2013, with World Bank support, the Ministry of Economy and Finance re-launched FMIS Phase 1 (2013-2016) at a smaller scale than the earlier 'Treasury System Project,' concentrating on implementation at the General Department of the National Treasury and the Capital-Provincial Treasury.

Commencement of FMIS Phase 1

FMIS was developed under a contract provided by FPT-FIS LG CNS Joint Venture, reaching completion in 2015. It underwent initial testing at key general departments. In 2016, the officially launched FMIS, equipped with six core modules, extended its functionality to all 25 capital-provincial treasuries.

FMIS Phase 2

FMIS has extended its coverage to include all 37 ministries and institutions, as well as fully delegated budget entities, along with the 25 capital-provincial Departments of Economy and Finance. In the years 2019-2020, the Strategic Plan Orientation for the New Implementation Procedures of FMIS and the Strategic Plan for Change Management were formulated to optimize the advantages of employing the system, aiming to minimize reliance on external procedures and paper documentation.

FMIS Phase 3

In addition to broadening the system's utilization across more national and sub-national administrative units, plans for the third phase of FMIS (2021-2025) have been developed. This phase is dedicated to reinforcing the system and ensuring its judicious use in alignment with the strategic plan for implementing the Public Financial Management Reform Program (PFMRP).

Vision

By 2030, FMIS will become a fully modern, integrated, and data‑driven Public Financial Management platform that enables timely, transparent, and evidence‑based decision‑making across all ministries and institutions.

Knowledge Related to PK-The Helper

PK-The Helper is a support system that addresses all issues and requests from FMIS users, offering effective and responsible solutions tailored to the level and specific situation of the problems users encounter.

System Access

To utilize PK-The Helper, users are required to have a username and password. Following this, they can open a web browser such as Google Chrome, Safari, Firefox and navigate to the following address: https://helpdesk.fmis.gov.kh .

Opening a Ticket

To initiate a support request in the system, users can submit issues or correction requests related to FMIS. The team will then promptly review and address these matters efficiently. Users can navigate to Ticket Categories, then to Ticket, next click Create, and click begin the process.

Search for Created Ticket and Track Status

Once a ticket, issue, request, or suggestion is created, users can track the progress of the solution provided by the team. This ensures timely access to resolutions and facilitates more effective coordination of work. Users can log in and navigate to Ticket Type, then click the Ticket to find the created ticket and track the ticket status.

Team Contact

If users encounter difficulties in creating or monitoring tickets or experience issues with the PK-The Helper system, they can contact the support team at 098 340 064.
